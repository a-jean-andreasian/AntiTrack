# AntiTrack

**An open-source tool that protects Article 8 of the ECHR - your right to private life.**

---

## The Problem

Tracking and surveillance have spiraled out of control. It's not just governments anymore. Any third-party startup with a web scraper can build a profile on you, package your data, and sell it to the highest bidder.

I'm not here to convince anyone. If you're comfortable posting your login credentials publicly or showering with the door wide open, this project isn't for you.

This tool is for people who are **fed up** with the shameless exploitation of personal information for profit.

---

## How It Works

AntiTrack doesn't block trackers - it **poisons** them.

Instead of trying to hide your digital footprint, we give trackers exactly what they want: data. But it's data that creates a **nonsense profile** - a constantly shifting identity that makes meaningful analysis impossible.

### The AntiTrack Approach

Your profile becomes a moving target:

- One moment you're a retired Canadian with no children who owns three cats
- The next, you're a middle-aged American parent of two
- Then, you're part of a young LGBT couple raising a toddler
- And so on - cycling endlessly

The goal isn't to feed random facts, but to create a **chain of contradictions** that makes drawing any conclusion impossible.

---
### Why This Works Against Modern AI

Today's classification AI systems are trained to find patterns. They rely on consistency - the assumption that a user *is* someone with stable attributes. AntiTrack exploits this by delivering:

- **Pattern denial** - constant shifts that prevent stable classification
- **Contradictory signals** - making confidence scores collapse
- **Unanalyzable noise** - profiles that can't be segmented or monetized

A mix of supply chain attack and malicious injection on tracker companies.
